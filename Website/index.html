My Inspirations:

  • Shadow of the Colossus

    Inspired me through its usage of the design by subtraction philosophy and the intrinsic, emotion driven narrative that is reinforced by its gameplay mechanics
  • Wong Kar Wai's filmography

    Inspired me through their unique cinematography and emotion driven narratives that tackle impactful and resonant themes
  • Journey

    Inspired me through its level design and the link it has to its intrinsic and player centric narrative
  • Perfect Blue

    Inspired me through the unique psychedelic viewing experience and the complex narrative that conveys important themes and messages in an interpretive way
  • Dark Souls

    Inspired me through its interconnected and well-planned level design as well as its polished combat experience that links to the games themes on a mechanical level instead of relying on external methods of storytelling
  • Studio Ghibli movies

    Inspired me through their unique art styles and the personally resonant and emotive themes they weave throughout
  • Ico

    Inspired me through its focus on companionship and the way its themes are expressed purely through gameplay, creating a resonant and deeply impactful experience
  • Metal Gear Solid Series

    Inspired me through how it conveys important and real world messages through game mechanics and narrative
  • Breath of The Wild

    Inspired me through its fresh take on open world design and its player driven experimental gameplay

Scrap Forge

University Project

Overall, level and camera design lead



"Scrap Forge" is an Isometric 3D Action-Adventure, Souls-like game set on a forgotten planet, collapsed and filled with junk.

Players take on the harsh world as a robot called "SCR4-P1" (also known as "Scrappy") who has crash landed on the desolate planet. Scrappy and by extension the player must explore and learn about the planet's past to find parts and repair their ship. Although this won't be easy, other robots whether being relics of the past or those who have also ended up trapped here scour the planet in search of fresh parts they can use to upgrade their now rusty exteriors.

Luckily, Scrappy is capable of crafting weapons, upgrades and supplies that the player can use in combat or to enhance their abilities. In a world where everything is against you and the odds of surviving are almost zero.

Can you get Scrappy home?

Scrap Forge was a collaborative project between the design, art and development cohorts at BCU serving as our final major project for our first year. On this project I was given the role of lead overall designer, lead level designer, and lead camera designer managing a team of 22 designers and the overall direction of the 70+ member large team across 3 months of development.

The Me in The Mirror

University Project

Lead overall designer and developer



Are you sure that's you in the mirror? The Me in the Mirror is an intense and uncomfortable psychological horror walking simulator placing players in the shoes of Daisy whilst she suffers from depersonalization-derealization disorder and attempts to recover from an abusive relationship.

Players will follow Daisy as she attempts to go through her daily routine split between the 4 levels, getting ready for work, travelling to work, working in an office and travelling back home as reality slowly crumbles around her. Haunted by her past abusive relationship and the DPDR it left her with gives Daisy and by extension the player no choice but to confront this deep-rooted trauma and her loosening tether on reality and her own self-perception.

The Me in the Mirror was a 2-month long university project I completed alongside 3 other designers and with some bug fixing help from a game programming student. It was our first time using Unreal Engine 5 and its blueprint visual scripting system providing us with many challenges to overcome. It was my job to help design the game and all its elements alongside developing a level of the game on my own with this being the first level of four.

Self Absorbed

Global Game Jam 2025

Designer



Bubble up and find a friend in a world so self-absorbed!

Self-Absorbed is a 3D puzzle adventure game about social isolation, unexpected companionship and of course bubbles. I and four other team members made Self Absorbed during Global Game Jam 2025.

Inspired by the works of Fumito Ueda this game takes a more intrinsic approach to storytelling and companionship through the innovative control scheme with players controlling two bubbles at once via the opposing joysticks. Players must use the differing abilities of both bubbles together to solve puzzles and traverse the dreamy world in search of a way to float into the sky with the rest of the bubble society.

Whilst the game is unfinished, we hope it demonstrates our desire to create a game that takes a more abstract and resonant approach to the theme of bubbles. Providing players with a game that explores the adverse effects of social bubbles and social isolation on those who cannot find a group they resonate and connect with especially in societies and social media landscapes that are so self-absorbed.
